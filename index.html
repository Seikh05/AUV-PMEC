01 — The Challenge

Why the Seabed?

Critical rare-earth elements powering the green energy transition are locked in hard-to-reach submarine deposits — demanding new, intelligent retrieval tools.

Rare earth elements (REEs) — spanning lanthanides and related critical minerals — are indispensable for EV motors, wind turbines, medical MRI machines, and defense systems. Global demand is projected to triple by 2040, yet terrestrial extraction faces intense geopolitical pressure and environmental constraints.

The seabed holds vast, largely untapped polymetallic nodule fields, cobalt-rich crusts on seamounts, and seafloor massive sulfide (SMS) deposits hosting cerium, neodymium, dysprosium, lanthanum, yttrium, and co-located cobalt, nickel, and manganese.

Current deep-sea mining concepts require billion-dollar investment. There is a critical unmet need for low-cost, modular prospecting platforms that can survey, classify, and selectively sample candidate sites before committing to large-scale extraction operations.

⚡ This AUV proposal addresses the prospecting gap — delivering multi-modal mineral sensing, targeted sampling, and georeferenced data logging at a fraction of the cost of industrial ROVs. Prototype v0.1 validates the concept in controlled shallow-water environments.

02 — The ROV Concept

AUV Architecture

A six-degree-of-freedom tethered ROV with modular payload bays, designed for iterative hardware upgrades without chassis redesign.

01
6-DOF Chassis
Aluminum-framed or HDPE-panel open-frame body with 6 brushless DC thrusters in a vectored configuration. Buoyancy balanced via syntactic foam inserts. Depth-rated to 50m in v0.1 with upgradeable housing for deeper variants.
02
Tri-Actuator End Effector
A hybrid claw-suction manipulator with three pneumatic/servo-driven fingers plus a central venturi suction port. Handles both loose sediment grab and attached crust fragment acquisition in a single tool assembly.
03
Sampling Chamber
Pressure-equalized cylindrical chamber with independent sample compartments. A motorized valve isolates collected specimens from water ingress. Capacity for 4 discrete sample types per dive, with depth-compensated sealing.
04
Sensor Suite
Dual-frequency forward-looking sonar, 4K downward-facing camera, NIR hyperspectral sensor, 3-axis magnetometer, and 9-DOF IMU. All sensors feed a centralized ROS2 sensor fusion pipeline in real-time.
05
Software Stack
ROS2 Humble middleware with MAVLink telemetry bridging. ArduSub firmware on Pixhawk flight controller manages thruster PID loops. Topside laptop/Raspberry Pi 5 runs the ground control interface and data logger.
06
Tether + Comms
Neutrally buoyant fiber-reinforced tether carrying 48V power and 100 Mbps Ethernet. Topside tether management spool with slip ring. Optional acoustic modem upgrade for untethered telemetry in deeper variants.
03 — End Effector

Tri-Actuator Hybrid Claw

A purpose-built manipulator combining mechanical grip with pneumatic suction — handling both unconsolidated sediment and hard crust fragments without tool swaps.

  • Mechanism Three servo-driven fingers at 120° radial spacing. Independent actuation allows pinch, scoop, and conform grips for irregular rock nodule geometry.
  • Suction Mode Central venturi suction cup powered by a miniature DC vacuum pump. Target vacuum pressure: 0.4–0.6 bar. Effective for loose sediment, fine-grained deposits, and small nodules ≤5 cm.
  • Force Budget Each finger servo rated for 15 kg·cm torque (waterproof MG996R or equivalent). Aggregate grip force estimated at 8–12 N on 30mm object diameter.
  • Materials 316L stainless steel linkages, Delrin (POM) knuckle joints, silicone finger pad inserts rated to 100m depth. Frame printed in PETG with CF reinforcement.
  • Control Interface ROS2 joint command topic, servo positions interpolated via PWM from Raspberry Pi GPIO or dedicated servo controller. Closed-loop force sensing via strain gauge in v0.2.
  • Sample Transfer Collected material deposited into the pressure-controlled sampling chamber via a funnel guide rail and motorized trap door — hands-free sample handoff cycle.
04 — Core Infrastructure

Pressure-Balanced Oil-Filled System

A lightweight, scalable alternative to full titanium enclosures, enabling cost-effective deep-sea system design by operating in a pressure-neutral state.

  • Core Concept Internal electronics are submerged in dielectric silicone oil. This allows internal pressure to completely match external ocean pressure via a bladder/piston compensator, eliminating structural stress.
  • Oil Functions Silicone oil acts as a near-incompressible pressure equalizer, a pristine electrical insulator, and a highly efficient thermal management medium for high-power subsea electronics.
  • Optics Isolation Because oil alters refractive indices, the camera module is isolated in a separate, rigid titanium housing with a sapphire window, maintaining internal atmospheric pressure.
  • Key Advantages Reduces the need for heavy, expensive pressure-resistant hulls. Lowers overall system weight, reduces fabrication costs, and improves reliability in deep environments (inspired by real offshore ROVs).
  • Engineering Needs Requires oil-compatible surface mount electronics, robust thermal management, subsea sealed cable-entry connectors, and a rigid framework to replace a traditional outer hull.
05 — Sensor Suite

Multi-Modal Sensing

Five complementary sensor modalities provide redundant mineral classification, enabling confident site scoring even in turbid or low-light seabed conditions.

Sonar
PING2 / Dual-Freq
BlueRobotics Ping2 single-beam sonar for bottom-lock altimetry. Optional DVL upgrade for velocity-aided navigation. Dual frequency (115 kHz / 460 kHz) separates sediment backscatter from hard-substrate returns.
Camera
4K UHD · 120fps
Wide-angle 4K underwater camera with adjustable LED ring illumination. Supports photogrammetric 3D reconstruction of surveyed sites using Structure-from-Motion post-processing. Video streamed over tether H.264.
NIR Spectral
AS7265x / μNIR
Near-infrared spectral sensing using AS7265x tri-spectrum IC (410–940nm, 18 channels). Identifies characteristic absorption bands of cerium, neodymium, and iron-oxide minerals. Housed in a depth-rated waterproof enclosure with sapphire window.
Magnetometer
QMC5883L · 3-axis
3-axis digital magnetometer detecting anomalies in Earth's field indicative of iron-rich or REE-bearing mineral concentrations. Cross-correlated with IMU heading to separate vehicle-induced magnetic noise from environmental signal.
IMU
ICM-42688 · 9-DOF
9-DOF inertial measurement unit providing 3-axis accelerometer, gyroscope, and magnetometer data at 1 kHz. Feeds EKF-based state estimator in ArduSub/ROS2. Critical for depth-hold, attitude stabilization, and dead-reckoning between GPS fixes.
Depth / Pressure
MS5837-30BA · ±2mm
High-resolution pressure and temperature sensor (0–300 m range, ±2 mm resolution). Provides absolute depth for sample provenance logging and serves as z-axis ground truth for the navigation filter and altitude-hold controller.
DVL (Future)
WL-21701 Upgrade Path
Doppler Velocity Log planned for v0.3. Enables centimeter-accurate bottom-relative velocity for precise station-keeping and survey-grade georeferencing without surface GPS. Designed as a bolt-on payload bay addition.
Acoustic Modem
WLP-M64 — v0.3+
Acoustic data link for tether-free operation at extended ranges. Enables multi-ROV mesh survey patterns and long-range telemetry in v0.3 deep-water variants. Current tether architecture is pre-wired for acoustic modem power + serial bridge.

EKF Sensor Fusion
Extended Kalman Filter fuses IMU, depth sensor, sonar altitude, and magnetometer heading into a single 15-state pose estimate. Runs at 200 Hz on Raspberry Pi 5 compute module. Dead-reckoning fallback during sonar dropouts.
MAVLink Integration
ArduSub firmware communicates over MAVLink v2 via mavros2 bridge. All vehicle states (attitude, depth, GPS, battery, mode) published as ROS2 topics. Control commands sent as SET_POSITION_TARGET_LOCAL_NED messages at 50 Hz.
Mineral Classification
NIR spectral readings processed by a lightweight CNN trained on REE mineral spectral libraries. Magnetometer readings processed by anomaly detection filter. Combined confidence score triggers the end effector sampling sequence autonomously.
06.5 — Core Systems

Technical Infrastructure

Robust subsystems engineered for reliable power, communication, and pressure tolerance in challenging environments.

Pressure System
  • Oil-Filled Balancing: Pressure-Balanced Oil-Filled (PBOF) design continuously equalizes internal and external pressure.
  • Structural Integrity: Drastically reduced structural stress, enabling deeper operation without heavy enclosures.
  • Titanium Housing: Critical optical sensors protected by high-strength, corrosion-resistant titanium enclosures.
Navigation & Comm
  • Sensor Suite: Integrated IMU, multiple SONAR arrays, and high-precision Depth sensors.
  • Sensor Fusion: Extended Kalman Filter (EKF) state estimation for robust, drift-free localization.
  • Telemetry: High-bandwidth tether connection combined with optional acoustic modem for tether-free data links.
Power System
  • Surface Power: High-voltage surface power delivered via umbilical tether for unlimited mission endurance.
  • Power Conversion: Highly efficient onboard DC-DC converters step down and regulate voltage for thrusters.
  • Battery Backup: Optional onboard lithium battery pack provides emergency power for safe return-to-surface.
07 — System Workflow

Mission Sequence

A repeatable six-stage dive cycle from surface deployment to data upload, designed for single-operator control with semi-autonomous sampling trigger logic.

1
Deploy
ROV lowered from vessel. Tether paid out. Depth-hold engaged at 2m. System self-check.
2
Survey
Sonar scanning, video capture, and magnetometer sweep. Waypoint-guided lawnmower pattern.
3
Detect
NIR + MAG anomaly scoring in real-time. Candidate sites flagged and georeferenced.
4
Sample
End effector triggered. Claw or suction mode selected. Sample deposited to chamber.
5
Ascend
Chamber sealed. ROV returns to surface via guided waypoint or tether tension detect.
6
Log
ROS2 bag synced to laptop. Sample metadata merged with GPS and depth logs. Report generated.

Prototype v0.1 — Modular Budget Demonstrator

The first prototype is a deliberately budget-conscious, modular demonstrator built for controlled shallow-water (≤15m) pool and coastal validation. Every subsystem is designed as a replaceable module, enabling iterative hardware upgrades without redesigning the chassis. This is the foundation, not the ceiling. Required Components

A comprehensive bill of materials spanning electronics, mechanical assemblies, and the software stack required to deploy the AUV prototype.

Electronics

  • Main Compute: Intel NUC or Raspberry Pi 4/5
  • Flight Controller: Pixhawk 4/6X (ArduSub)
  • Actuation: Waterproof high-torque servos (MG996R)
  • Power Mgmt: DC-DC Buck Converters & PDB

Mechanical

  • Arm Linkages: 316L Stainless Steel / Delrin Knuckles

  • End Effector: Tri-actuator silicone fingered grips
  • Sampling Chamber: Polycarbonate cylinders & active pistons
  • PBOF System: Flexible structural bladder housing compensator

Hardware

  • Camera Housing: Depth-rated Titanium enclosure
  • Optics: High-pressure polished Sapphire Window
  • Seals: Dual Viton rated O-Rings
  • Connectors: Subsea Wet-mate bulkheads
  • Tether: Neutral buoyancy fiber/copper umbilical

Software

  • Middleware: ROS 2 (Hardware interfacing/topics)
  • Flight Code: ArduPilot Custom / ArduSub (MAVLink v2)
  • Computer Vision: OpenCV & TensorFlow (Nodule Classification)
  • UI/UX: QGroundControl (Operator Control Station)
  • Estimation: EKF2 Sensor Fusion pipeline

Other Supplies
